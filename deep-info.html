Deeper Script Rationale

About Bradley

  • Bradley values dignity, respect, and being treated like an equal.
  • He values humour, honesty, and direct communication.
  • He does not like being bossed around.
  • Waiting and overload can increase frustration.

Why these scripts are brief

  • When Bradley is escalated, too much talking can make things worse.
  • Arguing or explaining systems can shift frustration onto staff.
  • Short, forward-moving language is more useful in the moment.
  • Redirecting to the next action reduces the chance of getting stuck in conflict.

Why not explain rules too much

  • Explaining “insurance”, “government rules”, or systems can become another point of argument.
  • It is often more effective to focus on the appointment, timing, transport, or next activity.
  • The aim is to reduce verbal clutter and keep momentum going.
Example: “Your appointment’s at 4pm. Let’s get going.”

Why use distraction and redirection

  • Distraction can interrupt fixation and reduce escalation.
  • Preferred topics or familiar banter can help him regulate.
  • Redirection works better than correction when he is already frustrated.

Why “courtesy transport” may work better

  • Some language may feel less confronting or less loaded for Bradley.
  • Courtesy transport or courtesy bus can feel more neutral.
  • The wording aims to support dignity and reduce resistance.

Proactive reminders for staff

  • Reduce waiting as much as possible.
  • Have the vehicle and equipment ready before movement starts.
  • Keep language short and concrete.
  • Use humour and warmth where appropriate.
  • Do not turn the interaction into a debate.
