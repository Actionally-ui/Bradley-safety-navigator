Prepare

Set context before transport or transfer.

Identify the transfer type

WAV transfer Manual transfer (non-WAV)

Manual transfer (non-WAV)

  • Follow OT plan and manual handling protocol.
  • Complete physical screening before moving.
  • Step 7 safety pause applies if risk escalates.

WAV transfer

  • Direct access only.
  • No manual transfer screening required.
  • General monitoring only.

Early signs to watch

  • Mumbling or slurred speech.
  • Removing footrests early.
  • Repetitive roster questions.

Early response

  • Distract and redirect.
  • Use banter or a preferred topic.
  • Check physical needs.

Live Decision

Use brief language. Keep moving forward. Avoid debates.

Agitation / escalation

“Bradley, we’ve got to head off now. Your appointment’s at [time]. Let’s get going.”
“The car’s here to take us. We’ll be quick and then we can [preferred activity].”
“Let’s head out now, we’re running on time.”

Staff reminders

  • Avoid explaining rules, systems, or insurance.
  • Do not argue or justify.
  • Keep language short and concrete.
  • Redirect to time, destination, or preferred activity.
  • If helpful, frame transport as courtesy transport or a courtesy bus.

When to move to Safety Pause

  • Attempting to stand without support.
  • Pushing wheelchair near roads or traffic.
  • High-volume shouting or cognitive overload.

Safety Pause

Manual transfers only. Stop, focus on safety, then choose the pathway.

Step 7

  1. Stop the transfer.
  2. Focus on safety.
  3. Use the decision tool below.
Pathway A: Skills deficit
  • Physical issue such as fatigue, balance, or confusion.
  • Bradley agrees to wait or change plan.
  • Outcome: duty of care.
“Bradley, your legs are too shaky today. It isn’t safe to transfer right now.”
Pathway B: Behaviour
  • Refusal, aggression, or unsafe actions.
  • Bradley attempts to proceed anyway.
  • Outcome: restrictive practice and reportable incident.

Important distinction

  • Pathway A = person agrees to wait.
  • Pathway B = person refuses and movement is restricted.
Go to Reporting ↓

Reporting

Record the pathway clearly and use the matching wording.

Pathway A

  • Record in shift notes.
  • Use wording: “Step 7 – Health/Safety Pause”.

Pathway B

  • Complete incident report.
  • Use wording: “Unauthorised Restrictive Practice”.

Duty of care vs restrictive practice

  • Duty of care: physical inability, person agrees, plan adjusts.
  • Restrictive practice: behaviour refusal, person does not agree, movement is blocked or restricted.

Quick Scripts

Short, repeatable phrases for staff use in real time.

Escalation / refusal

“Bradley, we’ve got to head off now. Your appointment’s at [time]. Let’s get going.”

Vehicle transition (WAV)

“The courtesy bus is here to take us to the cafe. Let’s head out.”

Mobility check (manual)

“Let’s just test the legs on the machine first.”

Distraction / rumbling

“How about those property prices in Currumbin? Did you see that boat on the news?”

Recovery & dignity

Preserve respect after the event.

  • Do not debrief the incident with Bradley.
  • Move straight to a preferred activity.
  • Act as if everything is normal to preserve dignity.
Core principle: Bradley values being the boss of his own life. Respect his identity, even in recovery.
