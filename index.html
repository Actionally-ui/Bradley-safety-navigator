Prepare

Before Transfer

Context Check

Identify the transfer type to know the rules.

Manual Transfer (Non-WAV)

  • OT Plan Required: Follow manual handling protocol.
  • Screening: Must pass physical check before moving.
  • Step 7 Applies: Safety Pause tool is active.
Requires high physical risk management.

WAV Transfer

  • Direct Access: No manual transfer protocol needed.
  • Step 7 N/A: Manual screening not required.
  • Baseline Safety: General monitoring only.

Zero Wait Rule

  • Vehicle arrived and doors open before Bradley moves.
  • Sara Stedy or wheelchair positioned before he stands.
  • Shoes / wallet ready before mentioning outing.

Live Decision

Early Signs (Rumbling)

Watch for the Cues

  • Mumbling or slurred speech.
  • Removing footrests early.
  • Repetitive roster questions.
  • Fixation on missing staff.

Staff Response

Distract and Redirect: Change the subject to a high-interest topic.

Check Physical Needs: Pain, toileting, or cognitive fatigue?

Use Banter: “Did you see that boat on the news?”

Escalation

Agitation Level

Signs of Escalation

  • Swearing or raised voice.
  • Accusing staff of laziness.
  • Firm refusal to enter vehicle.
  • Asserting control over roster or finances.

Use Direct Scripts

“Bradley, we’ve got to head off now. Your appointment’s at [time]. Let’s get going.”

“The car’s here to take us. We’ll be quick and then we can [preferred activity].”

“Let’s head out now, we’re running on time.”

Staff Actions

  • Reduce verbal clutter.
  • Give him space.
  • Stop arguing logic.
  • Wait for regulation.

Pathway A

Duty of Care (Skills Deficit)

Scenario

Bradley fails OT screening due to fatigue, disorientation, or poor balance.

Say This

“Bradley, your legs are too shaky today. It isn’t safe to transfer right now.”

Do This

Pause, postpone, or switch to an indoor / lower-demand activity if Bradley agrees.

Record This

“Step 7 – Health/Safety Pause”

Result

Not a Restrictive Practice

This is a negotiated duty-of-care decision based on a skills deficit.

Pathway B

Restrictive Practice (Behaviour)

Scenario

Bradley refuses to wait and attempts to leave anyway despite safety concerns.

Say This

“Bradley, it’s not safe to proceed right now. We need to pause.”

Do This

Maintain safety if Bradley refuses to wait and attempts to proceed anyway.

(Prevent exit, block door, lock gate as needed.)

Report This

“Unauthorised Restrictive Practice”

Result

Reportable Incident

Environmental restraint / containment. This limits his freedom of movement in response to behaviour.

Recovery & Dignity

After Incident

Core Principle

Bradley values being the boss of his own life. Respect his identity, even in recovery.

Staff Actions

✗

Do NOT debrief

Avoid discussing the incident with Bradley.
→

Move immediately

Go straight to a preferred, low-demand activity (coffee, TV).
✓

Act normal

Behave as if everything is normal to preserve his dignity.

Remember

  • Bradley may have no recollection of the escalation.
  • Fatigue and apology are normal recovery signs.
  • Respect his autonomy and identity.
  • Move forward without blame.
